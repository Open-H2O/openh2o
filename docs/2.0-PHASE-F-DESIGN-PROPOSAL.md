# Phase F — Apple Aesthetics Overhaul: Design Proposal

**Status:** PROPOSAL — awaiting Brent's reaction before any CSS is written.
**Date:** 2026-06-23
**Scope reminder:** Phase F is *purely aesthetic* — type, spacing, depth, colour,
motion. It changes no structure, layout, or behaviour. It is the final gate
before the `v2.0` tag (`docs/2.0-UX-PATTERN-SPEC.md`). It also absorbs the three
theme-level accessibility findings F2, F5, F6 (`docs/2.0-ACCESSIBILITY-AUDIT.md`).

This document is meant to be *reacted to*, not executed. It ends with three taste
decisions that are genuinely yours to make, each with a recommendation.

---

## The goal in one sentence

Make the web app read like the native Mac app: the same calm confidence that comes
from a deliberate type scale, generous and consistent spacing, surfaces that clearly
layer, a restrained accent, and motion that feels physical rather than mechanical.

## What's working today (don't break it)

- The near-black base (`#040608`) and the single-typeface discipline (Public Sans
  everywhere, tabular figures for numbers) are a strong, coherent starting point.
- The OKLCH data-viz ramps (furnace / reservoir / forest) are already perceptually
  even — that's the right model, we just extend it to the surface ladder.
- "Dense, not hidden" is a real product principle here and Phase F respects it.

## The three problems Phase F fixes

1. **Surfaces don't separate.** On the dashboard, the cards are nearly the same
   colour as the page behind them (`card #080b10` vs `base #040608`), and their
   borders sit at roughly **1.1:1** contrast — below the 3:1 the eye needs to read
   an edge. The cards are findable mostly because of their content, not their form.
   This is both an aesthetic gap and accessibility finding **F6**.

2. **Hierarchy leans on colour and size, not a system.** Gold does a lot of work
   right now — it's both the big stat numbers and the active-nav highlight. Apple's
   dark mode builds hierarchy primarily from **weight and size**, and spends accent
   colour sparingly so it stays meaningful.

3. **Contrast misses AA in two places.** The blue link colour in data tables
   (`#1B7FAF`, ~4.41:1) and the tertiary text colour (`#6b7f94`) both fall under
   the 4.5:1 floor on some surfaces. This is accessibility finding **F2** and it
   has to be fixed at the token level, which is exactly Phase F's job.

## The proposal, dimension by dimension

### 1. Type — hierarchy from weight and size, not colour

Keep Public Sans. Introduce a deliberate, named type scale so every screen pulls
from the same ladder instead of one-off font-sizes:

| Role | Size / line-height | Weight | Tracking | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Stat number (dashboard counts) | 34px / 1.0 | 700 | −0.02em | tabular-nums; tightened tracking is the single most "Apple" type move |
| Page title (`<h1>`) | 22px / 1.2 | 600 | −0.01em | |
| Section heading | 17px / 1.3 | 600 | 0 | |
| Body | 15px / 1.5 | 400 | 0 | generous line-height is the readability win |
| Label / eyebrow ("WATER DATA") | 11px / 1.2 | 600 | +0.06em uppercase | the widened tracking is what makes all-caps read as a label, not shouting |

The visible change: large text gets *tighter*, body text gets *more line-height*,
and labels get *wider* tracking. That combination is most of what makes type feel
considered rather than default.

### 2. Spacing — one rhythm, slightly more generous

The 4 / 8 / 16 / 24 / 32 / 48 / 64 scale is already good — the issue is consistency
and a little more breathing room at the structural seams. Concretely:

- Card interior padding: bump from the current value to `--space-lg` (24px) so
  content isn't tight against the edge.
- Gaps between cards and between sections: step up one notch where they're cramped.
- Everything snaps to the 8px grid — no stray 5px / 13px values.

This is where your **"dense, not hidden"** preference and Apple's generous
whitespace pull against each other. My recommendation is a *measured* loosening at
the structural level (cards, sections) while keeping data tables dense — see
Decision 3.

### 3. Depth — let surfaces actually layer (the big one)

Today there's a documented brand decision: *"Depth comes from the pop-shadow, not
from a lighter card"* — cards are the same `#080b10` as a near-black base and rely
on shadow alone. On the dashboard, that shadow isn't enough; the cards read flat.

Apple's dark mode does the opposite — each elevation step gets **both** a slightly
lighter surface **and** a soft shadow. The two cues reinforce each other.

I propose re-expressing the whole surface ladder in OKLCH so the lightness steps
are perceptually even, and giving cards a small but real lift:

| Token | Today | Proposed (OKLCH) | Effect |
|---|---|---|---|
| base | `#040608` | unchanged (~oklch 0.05) | near-black brand stays |
| card | `#080b10` | `oklch(0.13 0.012 260)` (~#16191f) | a whisper of lift so cards float |
| inset | `~#12151b` | `oklch(0.16 0.012 260)` | even step above card |
| elevated | `~#242a33` | `oklch(0.22 0.012 260)` | even step above inset |
| shadow | current pop-shadow | softer, larger-radius, lower-opacity | Apple-style diffusion |

This directly contradicts the existing "depth from shadow only" rule, so it's
**Decision 1** below — your call, because it's a brand decision, not a bug.

### 4. Colour — refine the palette, fix contrast (the locked-in part)

These three changes are **not optional** — they're the AA fixes (F2, F6) and they
have to land on the final tokens:

- **F2a:** Route text-sized blue — `.data-table-link`, `.text-blue`, `.badge-blue`
  — from `--color-blue` (`#1B7FAF`, 4.41:1) to `--color-blue-bright` (`#3DB4E0`,
  **8.26:1**). The darker blue stays for borders, fills, icons, large headings.
- **F2b:** Lighten `--color-text-tertiary` (`#6b7f94`) until it clears 4.5:1 on the
  worst surface (elevated). Target ≈ `#8a9bb0`; exact value verified at build.
- **F6:** Give *interactive* components (inputs, selects, buttons) a dedicated
  border token at ≥3:1 (≈ `rgba(120,160,200,0.32)`), separate from the decorative
  card-edge border which can stay subtle.

Beyond the fixes, the refinement is **accent restraint** — see Decision 2.

### 5. Motion — physical, fast, and respectful of "reduce motion"

Standardise on two motion tokens and apply them everywhere instead of ad-hoc
transitions:

- `--ease-standard: cubic-bezier(0.2, 0, 0, 1)` (an Apple-like ease-out)
- `--duration-fast: 150ms`, `--duration-base: 250ms`

And the **F5** fix — a global guard that nearly disables animation when the user's
OS asks for reduced motion:

```css
@media (prefers-reduced-motion: reduce) {
  *, *::before, *::after {
    animation-duration: 0.01ms !important;
    transition-duration: 0.01ms !important;
  }
}
```

## The three decisions that are yours

These genuinely change what I build, and they're matters of taste and brand, not
correctness. Everything in the "locked-in" colour section above happens regardless.

**Decision 1 — Depth.** Do we keep the documented "near-black cards, depth from
shadow only" rule, or let cards lift?
- *My recommendation:* a **whisper of lift** (the table above) — perceptible but
  still dark. It's the single biggest step toward the Mac-app feel and it fixes the
  "where's the card edge" problem. Full Apple-style light-gray cards would fight the
  brand; zero lift leaves the flat-card problem unsolved.

**Decision 2 — Accent restraint.** Gold currently does double duty: big stat
numbers *and* active-nav. Apple spends accent sparingly.
- *My recommendation:* make the **stat numbers near-white** (primary text) and
  **reserve gold for the active/selected state and key calls-to-action**. The
  numbers stay big and bold; they just stop competing with the nav for "the gold
  thing your eye jumps to." This is reversible if you hate it.

**Decision 3 — Density.** How far do we loosen spacing, given "dense, not hidden"?
- *My recommendation:* loosen at the **structural** level (card padding, section
  gaps) for calm, but keep **data tables dense** — that's where density is a feature,
  not a flaw. So: more air around the furniture, same information per screen in the
  tables.
