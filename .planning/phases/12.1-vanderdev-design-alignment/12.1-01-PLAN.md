<objective>
Bring OpenH2O's visual polish up to VanderDev standard by fixing surface contrast, section grouping, stat card treatment, and health card cleanup.

Purpose: The same VanderDev design tokens power both sites, but OpenH2O's card backgrounds are nearly invisible against the page surface (oklch L=0.08 vs base L=0.04, only 0.04 difference). VanderDev uses oklch L=0.17 for cards (0.13 difference, 3x more contrast). Fixing this plus adding section grouping labels will close the visual gap.
Output: Updated tokens.css, app.css, and ~10 templates. No Python changes.
</objective>

  <action>
Update these CSS custom properties in tokens.css to match VanderDev's surface contrast:

- `--color-card`: change from `#080b10` to `oklch(0.17 0.012 260)` (VanderDev measured value, 3x more contrast against base)
- `--color-elevated`: change from `#0e1219` to `oklch(0.21 0.012 260)` (proportional bump for inputs, dropdowns, overlays)
- `--color-hover`: change from `#141a22` to `oklch(0.24 0.012 260)` (proportional bump for hover states)
- `--color-inset`: change from `#050709` to `oklch(0.12 0.010 260)` (darker than card but lighter than base, for recessed elements)

Do NOT change: --color-base (same as VanderDev), --color-border (same as VanderDev), --color-border-hover, accent colors, text colors, OKLCH ramps, shadows, spacing, radius, or fonts.

Keep hex comments after oklch values for readability, e.g.:
`--color-card: oklch(0.17 0.012 260); /* ~#1a1e27 */`
  </action>
