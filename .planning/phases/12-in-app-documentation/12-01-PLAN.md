<task type="auto">
  <name>Task 2: Help pages (Getting Started walkthrough + Glossary)</name>
  <files>config/views.py, config/urls.py, templates/help/getting_started.html, templates/help/glossary.html, templates/partials/_sidebar.html</files>
  <action>
  1. Add two view functions to config/views.py:

     a) `getting_started(request)` - renders templates/help/getting_started.html. Requires login (@login_required).

     b) `glossary(request)` - renders templates/help/glossary.html with a context dict `terms` containing water accounting definitions. Requires login. Terms dict should include at minimum:
        - GSA: Groundwater Sustainability Agency, the local agency responsible for managing groundwater under SGMA
        - GSP: Groundwater Sustainability Plan, the 20-year plan each GSA must adopt
        - SGMA: Sustainable Groundwater Management Act (2014), the California law requiring groundwater management
        - Parcel: A plot of land identified by an Assessor Parcel Number (APN), the basic unit of water accounting
        - Well: A borehole used to extract groundwater, identified by state well number or local ID
        - Water Right: A legal entitlement to divert surface water, issued by the State Water Board
        - Point of Diversion (POD): The physical location where water is diverted from a stream or river
        - Managed Aquifer Recharge (MAR): Intentionally adding water to an aquifer through spreading basins or injection wells
        - Water Account: Groups parcels for accounting purposes, tracks supply and usage
        - Allocation Plan: Sets water budgets per zone and water type for a reporting period
        - Reporting Period: A time window (usually water year Oct 1 - Sep 30) for accounting
        - Ledger Entry: A double-entry record: supply amounts are positive, usage amounts are negative
        - GEARS: Groundwater Extraction Annual Report System, the DWR reporting format for per-well extraction
        - CalWATRS: California Water Transfer Reporting System, the Water Board format for surface diversions
        - CDEC: California Data Exchange Center, real-time hydrologic data from DWR
        - USGS: United States Geological Survey, provides stream gauge and groundwater level data
        - OpenET: Satellite-based evapotranspiration estimates, used to calculate crop water use
        - CIMIS: California Irrigation Management Information System, weather station data for agriculture
        - Data Source: An external agency or API that provides hydrologic measurements
        - Monitoring Station: A curated external sensor (stream gauge, weather station, groundwater well) linked to a data source
        - Health Check: Automated system diagnostic covering data freshness, connectivity, and configuration

  2. Add URL patterns to config/urls.py under a `help/` prefix:
     - `help/getting-started/` -> getting_started, name='getting_started'
     - `help/glossary/` -> glossary, name='glossary'

  3. Create templates/help/getting_started.html extending base.html:
     - Page title: "Getting Started"
     - Breadcrumb: Help / Getting Started
     - Page description: "A step-by-step guide to setting up your water accounting system."
     - Content: 8 numbered steps, each in a card-raised div with a section-header and 2-3 sentences of guidance:

       Step 1: Import Your Parcels - Upload parcel boundaries from your county GIS department as GeoJSON or Shapefile. Navigate to Parcels and use the import command. Each parcel needs an Assessor Parcel Number (APN).

       Step 2: Import Your Wells - Upload well locations from CSV or Shapefile. Each well needs coordinates and a state well number or local ID. Wells appear on the map and can be linked to parcels.

       Step 3: Set Up Water Accounts - Create water accounts to group parcels for tracking. Go to Setup > Accounts and create accounts for each management unit (farm, ranch, district zone).

       Step 4: Create a Reporting Period - Define the time window for accounting. Go to Setup > Periods. Most agencies use the water year (October 1 through September 30).

       Step 5: Enter Ledger Data - Record water supply (positive) and usage (negative) for each parcel. Enter manually or import via CSV. The ledger is the core of water accounting.

       Step 6: Connect Monitoring Stations - Link external data sources (CDEC stream gauges, USGS wells, CIMIS weather stations) to automatically pull measurements. Go to Stations > Add Station.

       Step 7: Add Water Rights and Recharge Sites - Record surface water rights with their points of diversion. Track managed aquifer recharge events at spreading basins or injection wells.

       Step 8: Generate State Reports - When your reporting period is complete, generate GEARS CSV (per-well extraction) and CalWATRS CSV (surface diversions) for submission to the Water Board. Go to Reports > Generate.

  4. Create templates/help/glossary.html extending base.html:
     - Page title: "Glossary"
     - Breadcrumb: Help / Glossary
     - Page description: "Water accounting terms used throughout the platform."
     - Content: Render terms alphabetically in a definition-list layout. Use a card-raised wrapper. Each term as a dt (styled bold, --color-text-primary) and definition as dd (--color-text-secondary, margin-bottom --space-md). Add an alphabetical jump-nav at top if >15 terms.

  5. Add a "Help" section to the sidebar (_sidebar.html) at the bottom, after the Setup section:
     - Section label: "Help"
     - Link: Getting Started (use a book/guide SVG icon)
     - Link: Glossary (use an A-Z/dictionary SVG icon)

  Avoid: Creating a new Django app for just two views (keep in config/). Avoid markdown rendering libraries. Do NOT add models (all content is in templates/views).
