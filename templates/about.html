OpenH2O

Open-source water accounting for California

OpenH2O is an open-source water accounting platform built for Groundwater Sustainability Agencies and water districts. It tracks groundwater extraction, surface water diversions, managed aquifer recharge, and mixed-use accounting in a single system, then generates the state reports agencies are required to file. The entire platform runs on a $15/month virtual private server and can be deployed by an AI coding assistant pointed at this repository.

Credits

California Water Data Consortium

The independent nonprofit founded in 2019 to carry out California's Open and Transparent Water Data Act (AB 1755, Dodd, 2016). As a neutral convener with no regulatory or taxing authority, the Consortium brings the Department of Water Resources, the State Water Resources Control Board, and the Department of Fish and Wildlife together with local agencies, Tribes, academia, and NGOs to make California's water data open and interoperable. With DWR, the State Water Board, and the Environmental Defense Fund, it stewards the open-source Groundwater Accounting Platform that OpenH2O is built upon.

Born of the 2012–2016 drought, which exposed how fragmented and inaccessible the state's water data had become. OpenH2O would not exist without the Consortium's work to make open water accounting the standard.
