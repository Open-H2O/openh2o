OpenH2O

Open-source water accounting for California

OpenH2O is an open-source water accounting platform built for Groundwater Sustainability Agencies and water districts. It tracks groundwater extraction, surface water diversions, managed aquifer recharge, and mixed-use accounting in a single system, then generates the state reports agencies are required to file. The entire platform runs on a $15/month virtual private server and can be deployed by an AI coding assistant pointed at this repository.

Open water data

California Water Data Consortium

The independent nonprofit founded in 2019 to carry out California's Open and Transparent Water Data Act (AB 1755). With no regulatory or taxing role of its own, it is the neutral table where the Department of Water Resources, the State Water Resources Control Board, and the Department of Fish and Wildlife sit with local agencies, Tribes, academia, and NGOs to make the state's water data open and interoperable.

The open-source Groundwater Accounting Platform that OpenH2O builds upon was co-developed by the Environmental Defense Fund and the Rosedale-Rio Bravo Water Storage District; the Consortium is the partner that, with those agencies, opened and scaled it for use statewide. Born of the 2012–2016 drought that exposed how scattered and locked-away California's water data had become, that work set the standard OpenH2O exists to put within reach of every agency.
