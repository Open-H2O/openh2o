Look up a public water system

A PWSID is the identifier the regulator carries for a public water system — CA1010001, for example. Enter it and this looks the system up in EPA's Envirofacts SDWIS record. Nothing is written until you review what came back and confirm.

What onboarding does and does not do

It creates the water system and its facilities from EPA's federal record, and re-running it against a system you already carry refreshes those same fields from EPA. That is the whole of it.

It does not determine compliance. EPA's SDWIS extract is a federal copy, not the primacy agency's authoritative file, and nothing here compares a system against a requirement.

It does not link facilities to the wells this platform meters. That join is a deliberate operator act, made in the Django admin, and an onboarding refresh never touches a link you have already made.

It does not fill in the population and service-connection breakdowns. EPA sends one total for each; splitting a total into residential, non-transient and transient shares would be inventing numbers, so those fields stay empty until a source that genuinely breaks them down is imported.
