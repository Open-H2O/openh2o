The short version

A water budget and an allocation are the same number seen from two directions. The budget is the whole pie — the total volume of water assigned to a management zone for a reporting period. The allocation is one account's slice of that pie, worked out from how much land the account holds in the zone. Set the budget at the top; the platform divides it into allocations underneath.

Allocation derived

One account’s slice — the platform pro-rates the budget by how many use areas the account holds in the zone.

Water budget — the cap you set

The water budget is the top-down decision. Your agency declares, for example, “this zone gets 12,000 acre-feet of groundwater this water year.” You set one budget per zone, per water type (groundwater, surface water, and so on), per reporting period. It is the policy ceiling for a whole area — the total volume available before anyone’s individual share is worked out.

An acre-foot is the volume of water covering one acre to a depth of one foot — roughly a year of household use. It is the standard unit California water managers count in.

Allocation — each account's share

A single landowner does not care about the zone’s full 12,000 acre-feet — they care about their limit. The platform calculates each account’s allocation by pro-rating the zone budget according to how many parcels (use areas) that account holds in the zone:

account allocation = zone budget × (account’s parcels in zone ÷ total parcels in zone)

Parcel count is used — not parcel acreage — on purpose: parcel-area data is often incomplete, and counts are always available. It trades a little precision for not breaking when data is missing.

A worked example

Say a zone has a water budget of 12,000 acre-feet for the year, and contains 600 parcels in total. Your account holds 30 of those parcels. Your allocation is:

12,000 × (30 ÷ 600) = 600 acre-feet

The platform then subtracts what the account actually used — the sum of its ledger entries (every metered extraction, surface diversion, and recharge credit) — to show what is left:

remaining = allocation − usage

That “remaining” figure is what turns a row red on the dashboard. When an account pumps past its allocation, remaining goes negative — that is an overdraft, and the only thing the platform colors red.

One thing worth knowing

If no water budget has been set for a zone in a given period, every account’s allocation shows as blank — not zero. “No budget defined” and “a budget of zero” are deliberately different: the platform will not imply a limit nobody actually set. To start enforcing allocations, set a water budget for the zone and period first on the Water Budgets page.
