Help / Getting Started

A step-by-step guide to setting up your water accounting system.

{% endblock %} {% block content %}
1

Import Your Use Areas

Upload use area boundaries from your county GIS department as GeoJSON or Shapefile. Navigate to Use Areas and use the import command. Each use area needs an Assessor Parcel Number (APN).

2

Import Your Extraction Wells

Upload extraction well locations from CSV or Shapefile. Each well needs coordinates and a state well number or local ID. Wells appear on the map and can be linked to use areas.

3

Set Up Water Accounts

Create water accounts to group parcels for tracking. Go to Setup > Accounts and create accounts for each management unit (farm, ranch, district zone).

4

Create a Water Year

Define the time window for accounting. Go to Compliance > Water Years. Most agencies use the water year (October 1 through September 30).

5

Enter Use Ledger Data

Record water supply (positive) and usage (negative) for each use area. Enter manually or import via CSV. The use ledger is the core of water accounting.

6

Connect Monitoring Stations

Link external data sources (CDEC stream gauges, USGS wells, CIMIS weather stations) to automatically pull measurements. Go to Monitoring Stations > Add Station.

7

Add Surface Diversions and Recharge Areas

Record surface water rights with their points of diversion. Track managed aquifer recharge events at spreading basins or injection wells.

8

Generate State Reports

When your reporting period is complete, generate GEARS CSV (per-well extraction) and CalWATRS CSV (surface diversions) for submission to the Water Board. Go to Reports > Generate.
